Maison • Branding + UI Color Stories

Three separate warm-home directions for Maison

Prepared for Victor and Riah. These directions are meant to replace the current glossy, synthetic palette with something that feels warmer, cleaner, more lived-in, and more intentional.

“Maison should feel like a beautiful, cared-for home, not a generic software dashboard.”
This document turns that instinct into three distinct visual routes.

What changed

The current palette reads cool, glossy, and a little default-AI. These new routes are built around warmth, restraint, domestic texture, and emotional clarity.

What stays true

Maison still needs clear interaction states, strong contrast, and enough freshness to feel current. Warm should never mean muddy or sleepy.

Color Story 01

Sunwashed Market

Cream, mustard, teal, and walnut. Bright, tasteful, alive, and unmistakably human.

This direction feels like morning light in a warm kitchen, handmade ceramics on open shelves, and a home that is cared for without looking precious. It keeps Maison friendly and optimistic, but pulls it far away from glossy purple SaaS.

Palette
Antique Cream#F7F1E5
Butter Linen#EFE3CD
Harvest Mustard#D0A84A
Kitchen Teal#4D8C86
Walnut#4B392D
Branding
  • Best for a Maison that feels clean, capable, warm, and a little editorial.
  • The mustard gives it memorability. The teal keeps it calm and believable.
  • This is the strongest fit if the brand should feel modern, lived-in, and a little premium without turning stiff.
UI
  • Use cream and butter as the main canvas so the app breathes.
  • Reserve mustard for primary actions, highlighted numbers, and confident moments.
  • Use teal for progress, checks, navigation focus, and functional accents.
  • Keep walnut for headings, anchors, and contrast zones instead of black.
Type
Typography should feel tailored, not futuristic. I would pair a warm, strong wordmark with a clean humanist sans in the UI. For a custom Maison wordmark, I would explore soft flared terminals, slightly high contrast, generous spacing, and elegant capitals that still feel domestic rather than cold-luxury.
Avoid
Avoid neon teal, sharp lemon yellow, cold gray backgrounds, or overusing the mustard. The charm is in restraint.

Color Story 02

Sage Linen

Linen, oat, sage, olive, and muted gold. Quiet confidence, calm order, and soft daylight.

This direction is the most understated. It feels like expensive soap packaging, warm stone countertops, natural fiber curtains, and a house that is calm because someone actually thought about it. It is less playful than Sunwashed Market, but very believable.

Palette
Linen#F6F1E8
Oat#ECE3D5
Soft Sage#8F9A77
Olive Shade#6B7656
Muted Gold#C2A46A
Branding
  • Best for a Maison that wants to feel mature, composed, and trustworthy.
  • This one says thoughtful home care rather than energetic startup.
  • It is the safest premium direction if you want warm neutrality to lead the brand.
UI
  • Use linen and oat for nearly all app backgrounds.
  • Let sage carry buttons, selected states, and positive system moments.
  • Bring muted gold in carefully for premium callouts, pricing, and highlights.
  • Use olive for utility depth and secondary emphasis.
Type
This story wants typography that feels composed and grounded. The custom Maison wordmark here should be more restrained, with subtle contrast, open counters, and a classic silhouette that can age well. Think modern heritage rather than trendy DTC.
Avoid
Do not let this become muddy. Too much olive or too little contrast will make it feel sleepy instead of calm.

Color Story 03

Clay Hearth

Oat, parchment, terracotta, cedar, and espresso. Warmth, depth, texture, and strong domestic character.

This direction has the most personality. It feels like fired clay, wood, books, old house character, and a little ritual. If Maison should feel soulful and memorable, this is the boldest route without becoming noisy.

Palette
Parchment#F8F1E8
Warm Oat#EEE0D1
Terracotta#B46F54
Cedar#7D523C
Espresso#34241D
Branding
  • Best for a Maison that should feel distinctive, emotional, and rich.
  • This one creates the clearest personality and the strongest visual memory.
  • It is warmer and more intimate, which could be powerful if the product story leans toward belonging and care.
UI
  • Use parchment and oat as the quiet foundation.
  • Use terracotta for hero actions, primary calls to action, and emphasis moments.
  • Use cedar for tabs, filter states, and structured navigation depth.
  • Use espresso for text, framing, and dark panels instead of default charcoal.
Type
This story wants the strongest display typography. The custom Maison wordmark could push furthest here, with elegant tension between softness and structure. I would explore slightly sculpted serifs, gentle flare shapes, and a bespoke capital M that feels architectural but warm.
Avoid
Do not combine this with generic app-store blue or shiny gradients. It needs matte, tactile handling.

Recommendation

What I would test first

Best first test

Sunwashed Market is the best first move. It is the clearest break from the current AI-looking palette, but it still feels fresh, useful, and warm instead of heavy.

Safest premium route

Sage Linen is the calmest and most composed. If you want Maison to feel quiet, mature, and trustworthy first, this is the safest premium direction.

Strongest personality route

Clay Hearth has the most soul. If Maison should feel emotionally distinctive and memorable, this is the boldest route.

Font next

Yes, I can absolutely design the custom Maison font direction. My recommendation is to start with a custom Maison wordmark and display alphabet first, then decide whether it should expand into a full font family. That gets us the emotional signature fast without taking on the full production burden too early.
