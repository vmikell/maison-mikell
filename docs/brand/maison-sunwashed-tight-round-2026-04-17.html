Maison • Tighter Round

Refining the leading direction before we move into typography

Sunwashed Market is still the strongest overall, so this deck tightens that direction instead of starting over. The point here is to figure out what kind of warmth it should have when it gets more intentional.

“Before we move onto typography.”That means the visual direction itself should get sharper first. These are three tighter Sunwashed Market variants, each still living in the same family but pulling the emotional balance a little differently.

What stays fixed

The family stays cream, mustard, teal, and walnut. The question is what leads: brightness, refinement, or depth.

What I’m testing

How far Maison can move toward premium-home identity without losing clarity, friendliness, and daily usability.

Recommendation

What I would carry forward

Best overall next step

Balanced Warm is the strongest path if we want Maison to feel distinctive, warm, and still easy to trust in product form. It is the safest path into a real brand system.

Most elegant

Brighter Editorial is the prettiest and lightest, but it loses a little of Maison’s grounding.

Most brand-rich

Richer Walnut is the most emotionally interesting, and it may become the best long-term identity once the typography gets stronger.

My recommendation

I would keep Balanced Warm as the production-leading direction, while borrowing a little of the walnut depth from Richer Walnut when we move into typography and final brand polish.
