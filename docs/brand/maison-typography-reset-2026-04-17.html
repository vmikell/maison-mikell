Maison • Typography Reset

Resetting the wordmark direction around Tailored House, with spacing handled properly

You and Riah were right to push back. The earlier type round had the right general territory, but it was not tight enough. This pass treats Tailored House as the useful baseline and focuses on spacing, rhythm, and restraint instead of trying to force personality too early.

The closest useful signal was Tailored House.So this deck stops trying to win with flourish. It tries to win with proportion, breath, and confidence.

What changed

Less decorative styling, less forced personality, more attention to spacing, stroke rhythm, and how the wordmark actually sits on a page.

What I’m judging now

Which version feels like Maison could live with it for years, not which one gets the fastest reaction in a vacuum.

Recommendation

What I would push next

Best overall

Tailored House, close-set is the strongest next move. It feels the most intentional without getting precious or generic.

Best if the palette gets richer

Tailored House, warmer joints becomes more interesting if you lean toward Richer Walnut or a deeper final brand system.

What I learned

The problem was not just style, it was spacing. The earlier round tried to express too much through shape without locking the rhythm first.

Recommendation

I would build the next typography pass from Tailored House, close-set, then warm it very carefully rather than starting from the more expressive side.
