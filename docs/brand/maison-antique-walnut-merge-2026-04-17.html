Maison • Merged Direction

Antique Hearth merged with Richer Walnut

This merge keeps the warmth and domestic feel of Antique Hearth, but borrows the stronger structure and deeper grounding from Richer Walnut. It is less soft than Antique Hearth on its own and less formal than Richer Walnut on its own.

This is probably the real synthesis.Antique Hearth had the better emotional warmth. Richer Walnut had the better brand discipline. Together they feel closer to a final Maison direction than either one alone.

What stays

Antique, Natural Cotton, Terracotta, Gold Trimming, and Leather remain the core palette.

What changes

Leather leads more of the structure, Terracotta becomes more intentional, and the overall layout feels more controlled and premium.

My take

What improved

The warmth now feels less tentative, and the dashboard has more backbone. This is much closer to a final product identity than Antique Hearth alone.

What to watch

Leather should carry structure, not take over the whole surface. Gold should remain trim, and terracotta should be the emotional accent, not the background default.

Recommendation

This is the strongest combined direction so far. If you want one palette path to keep refining, I’d put this ahead of the separate Antique Hearth and Richer Walnut branches.
