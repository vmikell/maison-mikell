Maison • Typography Round

Tighten Tailored House, then test three cleaner sans-serif routes beside it

This pass does exactly what you asked: one tighter Tailored House baseline, plus three other sans-serif directions built seriously instead of thrown in as filler. The point is not novelty, it is to see what actually holds Maison together best.

The question is no longer whether Tailored House was the strongest signal. It was.Now the useful test is whether a tighter Tailored House still wins once it is put beside three modern sans-serif options with equally controlled spacing.

What I’m testing

Warmth, control, and whether the wordmark feels like a home brand instead of a generic app or fashion label.

What I tightened

Tracking, internal rhythm, and how the mark sits in real landing and product copy, not just on a blank stage.

Recommendation

What I would carry forward

Best overall

Tailored House, tighter still wins. It has the most Maison character and now the spacing is finally doing it justice.

Best sans-serif challenger

Plus Jakarta Sans is the strongest modern alternative if you want to test a cleaner route without going cold.

What this clarified

Maison can wear a sans-serif, but the more purely modern the type gets, the more the brand starts losing its home-specific emotional read.

Recommendation

I would keep pushing Tailored House, tighter as the lead, with Plus Jakarta Sans as the only sans-serif direction worth carrying into another serious pass.
