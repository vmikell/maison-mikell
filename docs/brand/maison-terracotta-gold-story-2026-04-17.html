Maison • New Color Story

Terracotta, gold, and leather as a warmer Maison direction

Built from the palette Riah just sent over. This is a more grounded, richer Maison story that uses the terracotta, gold, and brown as the emotional core, while keeping Antique and Natural Cotton as the breathable base.

This one makes immediate sense.The palette already has hierarchy: Antique for light, Natural Cotton for softness, Terracotta for warmth, Gold for polish, and Leather for anchor. That’s a real color story, not just a set of nice chips.

Why it works

It feels warm and expensive without going glossy. It also solves the flatness issue because the dark brown gives the system a real anchor.

How I’d use it

Antique and Natural Cotton carry the space, Terracotta leads emotional moments, Gold handles premium accents, and Leather creates authority.

My take

What this solves

This gives Maison a warmer, more confident center without the earlier flatness or the more generic startup energy.

What to watch

Do not let terracotta and gold both shout at once. Gold should feel like trim, exactly like the palette calls it, and Leather should do a lot of the grounding.

Recommendation

This is worth sitting next to Richer Walnut. It feels a little more domestic and a little less formal, which might be exactly the right Maison balance.
