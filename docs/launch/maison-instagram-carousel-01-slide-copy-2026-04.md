# Maison Instagram Carousel 01, Final Slide Copy

Prepared: 2026-04-18

## Post Goal

Make people feel instantly recognized by the small repeated frictions of shared-home life, then position Maison as the calm fix.

## Format

- 7 slides
- warm, premium, domestic visual tone
- minimal copy per slide
- no heavy product UI until the final slide

## Cover

### Main line
**7 tiny home frictions that create way more stress than they should**

### Optional sub-line
The stuff that quietly makes shared-home life feel heavier.

---

## Slide 2

### Headline
**Forgetting what needs to be bought**

### Body
You notice it only when you are already out of it.
And somehow no one knows who was supposed to remember.

### Design note
Use a grocery or pantry visual with a little empty-space tension.

---

## Slide 3

### Headline
**One person carrying the reminder load**

### Body
Appointments. refills. tasks. follow-ups.
When all of that lives in one person's head, the system is already broken.

### Design note
Keep this one emotionally simple and slightly heavier.

---

## Slide 4

### Headline
**Maintenance tasks living nowhere useful**

### Body
Filters. batteries. small repairs. routine upkeep.
Nothing feels urgent, until suddenly it is.

### Design note
Home-maintenance visual, but keep it elegant, not hardware-store energy.

---

## Slide 5

### Headline
**Repeating the same conversation every week**

### Body
Did we get that?
Who is handling this?
I thought you had it.

### Design note
This slide can be the most textural or conversational one.

---

## Slide 6

### Headline
**Everything living in five different places**

### Body
Texts. notes. memory. screenshots. the calendar.
A shared home does not feel calm when the system is scattered.

### Design note
Visually collage-like, but still clean.

---

### Body

## Caption, refined draft

Most home stress does not show up as one huge disaster.

It shows up as tiny repeated misses.
The grocery thing.
The reminder thing.
The maintenance thing.
The "I thought you had it" thing.

That is the kind of friction Maison is built to reduce.

Not by turning home life into a productivity competition.
Just by giving shared-home life one calm place to live.

If this feels familiar, Maison will probably make sense to you.

## Short caption version

A lot of shared-home stress is tiny, repetitive, and weirdly draining.
Maison is being built for exactly that layer.
