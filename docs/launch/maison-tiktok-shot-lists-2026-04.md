# Maison TikTok Shot Lists

Prepared: 2026-04-18

These shot lists are built for low-friction production.
They assume a founder-led or creator-like style, fast edits, natural light when possible, and domestic realism over polished ad energy.

## General shooting rules

- open with motion or tension in the first second
- keep clips short, usually 0.5 to 2.0 seconds
- mix hands, objects, rooms, and occasional face-to-camera footage
- do not overuse direct app screen recordings
- treat the app as the resolution, not the whole story

---

# TikTok 01

## Hook
**If living together keeps producing the same tiny arguments, it might not be a relationship problem. It might be a system problem.**

## Shot list
1. quick messy counter or fridge moment
2. face-to-camera hook delivery
3. close-up of half-written grocery note
4. phone text thread or notes app glimpse
5. cabinet / pantry reveal showing something missing
6. hand gesturing at a calendar or reminder
7. face-to-camera line: "too many places, no clear flow"
8. clean, calm home detail
9. brief Maison app glimpse or neutral product hint
10. end card or spoken CTA

## Edit rhythm
- strongest tension in first 2 seconds
- cut every 1 to 1.5 seconds early
- slow slightly at the resolution line

## On-screen text beats
- tiny arguments
- bad system
- too many places
- no clear flow
- home should feel calmer

# TikTok 02

## Hook
**The grocery issue is almost never really about groceries**

## Shot list
1. empty shelf or missing ingredient shot
2. hand opening fridge and realizing something is gone
3. direct-to-camera hook line
4. text thread / note / mental list style visual
5. one person remembering, one person assuming type beat
6. shopping bag or checkout clip
7. slight annoyed domestic moment, nothing too theatrical
8. line about friction adding up
9. calmer kitchen or table shot
10. product hint / CTA

## Edit rhythm
- very fast opening
- keep it conversational, not dramatic
- let the middle breathe for one beat when saying "friction adds up"

## On-screen text beats
- not really groceries
- shared info is everywhere
- one person remembers
- one person assumes
- friction adds up

# TikTok 03

## Hook
**Three tiny things that make a home feel more coordinated immediately**

## Shot list
1. warm home establishing shot
2. face-to-camera opener
3. one shared shopping system, grocery list or pantry restock visual
4. one place for recurring home tasks, simple home-maintenance visual
5. one place for reminders, calendar / reminder / sticky note contrast visual
6. face-to-camera line: "this changes the emotional feel fast"
7. calm domestic reset moment, table, counter, or tidy room
8. Maison product glimpse
9. CTA end frame

## Edit rhythm
- cleaner, slightly calmer pacing than TikTok 1 and 2
- make each of the three points visually distinct

## On-screen text beats
- 1 shared shopping system
- 1 place for home tasks
- 1 place for reminders
- less guessing
- less repeating
- calmer home

# TikTok 04

## Hook
**Things that feel normal at home but are actually signs your system is broken**

## Shot list
1. face-to-camera hook
2. repeated reminder text or sticky note visual
3. person texting themselves what the house needs
4. quick shrug / uncertainty moment about who is handling what
5. small maintenance issue visual
6. line: "a lot of people think this is just adult life"
7. face-to-camera response: "some of it is, some of it is just bad coordination design"
8. calm closing shot
9. Maison hint / CTA

## Edit rhythm
- hook should feel slightly provocative
- middle should feel like fast recognition beats
- closing should land with confidence, not sales pressure

## On-screen text beats
- feels normal
- system is broken
- repeating reminders
- nobody knows who has it
- bad coordination design

# Fast production checklist

Before posting, confirm:
- the hook lands in under 2 seconds
- the video can be understood muted with text on
- there is a real emotional or practical tension
- Maison appears as relief, not as an intrusive app ad
- the ending CTA is simple and natural
