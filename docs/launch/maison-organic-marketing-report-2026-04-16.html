1. The marketable story

Maison’s edge is emotional clarity, not feature sprawl. The marketing story has to make the product feel like relief, not just productivity.

What Maison is

  • A calm shared system for running a home.
  • A product for couples and households, not just solo list-making.
  • A place for shopping, maintenance, reminders, and household planning to live together.

What Maison is not

  • Not another generic family organizer.
  • Not a hyper-optimized productivity app.
  • Not a feature blob trying to be everything for everyone.

Primary audience

  • Couples sharing a home.
  • Busy homeowners.
  • Households feeling recurring coordination friction.
  • People who want calm more than hustle-culture productivity.

Hero message

The home operating system for couples.

Maison brings maintenance, shared shopping, reminders, and household planning into one clean place, so running a home feels lighter instead of chaotic.

“The first job is not to reach everyone. The first job is to make the right households say: this actually makes our home life easier.” Guiding launch principle for Maison

5. Content system

Maison content should feel aesthetic, emotionally sharp, and specific. The goal is not to “post often,” it is to build the right signal.

Content pillars

  • Shared-home friction
  • Calm systems for couples
  • Shopping and maintenance routines
  • Founder perspective
  • Soft product demos

Formats

  • Short videos
  • Carousel posts
  • Static quote-style graphics
  • Founder notes
  • Launch DMs and outreach

Tone rules

  • Calm, not loud
  • Premium, not sterile
  • Domestic, not corporate
  • Specific, not generic
  • Warm, not cheesy
Weekly rhythm 1 hero post, 2–3 supporting social posts, 1 founder-style note, and a small batch of warm outreach messages.
Creative rule Every post should either sharpen desire, validate the problem, or make the product feel more real.
Feedback loop Track saves, shares, clicks, objections, and repeated questions, then update copy and onboarding from what people actually respond to.
